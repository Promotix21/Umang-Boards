## Register

register: brand

## Users

**Primary user:** Purchase manager or technical head at a transformer or electrical equipment manufacturer in India or overseas, visiting the site to qualify UBL as a supplier. On desktop. Comparing quality signals: certifications, manufacturing scale, product range.

**Secondary user:** International buyer or distributor doing pre-qualification research, looking for export credentials, NABL certifications, and company credibility.

**Device split:** 70% desktop, 30% mobile

**Mindset at arrival:** Due diligence — not browsing, qualifying. Needs to see authority, scale, and trust signals immediately.

## Brand

**Voice:** Industrial authority — precise, confident, Made in India pride

**Tone:** Serious and assured, never flashy, never casual. The language of engineering excellence.

**Personality in 3 words:** Authoritative, Precise, Enduring

**What this brand is NOT:** Not a startup, not a consumer brand, not generic B2B grey

## Anti-References

1. Generic industrial manufacturer sites — grey backgrounds, stock machinery photos, Times New Roman, no visual hierarchy
2. Cheap Indian B2B directories — cluttered, no whitespace, spec tables without design
3. Navy-and-gold cliché done poorly — dark background with yellow text, no breathing room, feels dated

## Inspiration References

1. [taste.so research] — premium B2B manufacturer sites that feel engineered, not decorated
2. [taste.so research] — industrial brands with strong editorial identity (Herzog & de Meuron, Rolls-Royce industrial divisions)
3. Emil Kowalski reference — precise, restrained hover states that feel mechanical and intentional

## Color Strategy

- [x] **Committed** — Navy (#0B1F3A) anchors the brand; Gold (#C8A84B) used for accents and hierarchy signals, never decoration

## Theme Justification

Scene: "A procurement engineer at a German transformer company opening the site on a 27-inch monitor in a well-lit office, evaluating Indian suppliers for a long-term contract. The site must feel as serious as a Siemens technical document."

Conclusion: Light with dark sections — white base for readability, dark navy sections for authority moments

## Domain Reflex Check

Generic "industrial manufacturer India" site looks like: white-grey background, product photo grid, spec tables, green or orange accent colors, Poppins everywhere, trust badges clustered at the bottom

This site will instead: editorial navy and gold identity, precision typography, manufacturing scale shown through photography and numbers, certifications foregrounded, feels like a premium European industrial brand made in India

## Strategic Principles

1. Certifications above the fold — NABL and other credentials are the primary trust signal for B2B buyers
2. Scale communicates credibility — factory footage, capacity numbers, export countries
3. Gold is earned — only used for genuine hierarchy moments (key stats, section dividers, CTAs)
4. About Us must tell the manufacturing story — founding, growth, Made in India pride, leadership faces
5. No decorative gold — every gold element must mean something

## Conversion Goals

**Primary CTA:** Contact for product enquiry / Request a quote
**Secondary CTA:** Download product catalogue
**What success looks like:** Buyer adds UBL to their shortlist and initiates contact
